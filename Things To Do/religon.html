Al-Azhar University

was founded in 975 AD by the Fatimids in Egypt, which was nicknamed the Fatimids in relation to the youngest daughter of the Prophet Muhammad Fatima al-Zahra. The university was named in her honor

Bab Zuweila

was built in 485 AH / 1092 AD, was a site of execution during the Mamluk era, and is now the only surviving southern gate to the medieval city of Cairo. There are many interesting exhibits on the history of the gate with accurate explanations

The Muhammad Ali Mosque

The Muhammad Ali Mosque is located inside the Citadel of Salah al-Din al-Ayyubi in Cairo. This mosque was built during the reign of Muhammad Ali Pasha in 1848 AD on the site of the Mamluk palaces. It is considered the most prominent mosque with an archaeological character in Egypt.

The Sultan Hassan Mosque

the Sultan Hassan Mosque is the Islamic school of Egypt and the most famous monument in the Islamic world.The Sultan Hassan School or Mosque, this gigantic edifice, was founded by Sultan Hassan, son of the great Mamluk Sultan Al-Nasir Muhammad bin Qalawun in 1361

St. Catherine's Monastery

St. Catherine's Monastery was built in 565 to house the monks who were settlers in the Sinai Peninsula. The monastery is located on Mount Catherine in the Sinai. It has a library that includes the largest collection of books, about 3,500 books in different languages. It is named after St. Catherine and was founded by Justinian I. Emperor

The Museum of Islamic Art

The Museum of Islamic Art houses the largest and most impressive collection of Islamic antiquities in the world. The museum building was established in 1880 and includes the largest collection of pieces from all over the Islamic world, which allows the visitor to roam through all periods of Islamic history

St. George's Church

It is a Greek Orthodox church located in the Citadel of Babylon in Old Cairo, and it is affiliated to the Patriarchal Monastery of St. George the Patriarch of the Greek and Orthodox Patriarchate in Alexandria and All Africa. The church was built in the tenth century AD.

The monastery of Saint Simeon

is located on the right bank of the Nile in Aswan. It was built in 700 AD and is still in good condition, although it is located in an abandoned place. You can now discover the Coptic murals and enjoy them after their restoration and spend a good time in worship.
