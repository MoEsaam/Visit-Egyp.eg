More About Safari

The safari is a style of land trekking started by European travelers and explorers in Africa in the eighteenth century, usually using local African guides, for a variety of purposes such as

  • - Tourism
  • - Watching wild animals
  • - Enjoying the African wilds
  • - camping lifestyle

Saint Catherine's Mountains

Mount Catherine or Mount St. Catherine is located in the South Sinai Governorate and is considered one of the highest mountains in Sinai, with a height of 2.629 m above sea level. Snow falls on the mountain in the winter, like the rest of the mountains of the South Sinai

Siwa Oasis

Siwa Oasis is considered one of the areas rich in distinguished tourism potentials, including safari tourism. It covers an area of about 7800 square kilometers and is 800 square kilometers away from Cairo. It is characterized by sandboarding, similar to "snowboarding".

Mousa's Mountain

It is located in South Sinai Governorate. Its height is 2,285 meters. It was named Mount Moses after the Prophet Moses, who was spoken to by his Lord on this mountain, and who received the Ten Commandments

Ras-mohamed

Located in South Sinai, it is the second most important nature reserve in the world. It is an integrated embodiment of an ecosystem that includes the sea with its blue waters, mountains, rocks and plants.

White Desert

It is located in the new valley. Its importance is due to being an open museum for studying desert environments, geographical phenomena, fossils and wildlife, as well as containing antiquities and tools dating back to prehistoric times.

Jalf El-Kebeir

The "Julf Al Kabeer" area is located on an area of 7,770 km of limestone and sandstone, at an altitude of 300 meters above the surrounding desert, interspersed with valleys, most notably Wadi Al-Talh, Wadi Abd al-Malik and Wadi al-Hamra. international environment

Owainat's Mountain

Jabal Al-Owainat (1934 meters) is a mountain range in the Libya-Egypt-Sudan border region, covering an area of ​​1,500 square kilometers. The area is known for its ancient primitive drawings on mountain rocks.
