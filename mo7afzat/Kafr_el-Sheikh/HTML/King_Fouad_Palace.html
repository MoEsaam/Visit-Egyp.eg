King Fouad Palace



Kafr El-Sheikh Governorate is one of the governorates that possesses a large amount of antiquities, whether Pharaonic or Islamic, including King Fouad's Palace and its annexes in the city of Kafr El-Sheikh.

King Fouad used to place Kafr El-Sheikh in a special place, and the evidence for this is the historical evidence that King Fouad left in Kafr El-Sheikh, which was called the Al-Fouadiya Directorate in relation to him.

The first of these evidences left by the king (the palace of King Fouad I): This palace was lived in by the king while he and his family were in Kafr El-Sheikh, and it is in the European style (Italian-French) and the date of its establishment dates back to 1934 AD. The palace consists of two floors and is considered an urban masterpiece. civilized.

Recently, a decision was issued to convert King Fouad's Palace in Kafr El-Sheikh into a monument and consider it a tourist attraction and turn it into a history museum where princely collections and royal jewelry from different eras are displayed.

This palace has extensions, the royal railway station, which belonged to the king and his family and is located behind the palace, in addition to the kitchen, water tanks and the building of a water-lifting machine.. The king used to perform the rituals of prayer during his stay in Kafr El-Sheikh.

The king also left in the city of Kafr El-Sheikh the building that was intended for the management of private property, and then used it as a headquarters for the inspection of the endowments of Kafr El-Sheikh.

In 1961, this building was used as the headquarters of the General Office of Kafr El-Sheikh Governorate after its separation from the Gharbia until it became an independent governorate under local rule. In 1964, the governorate moved to its new building and this building was handed over to the Directorate of Education.

It was then decided to convert this building to be the headquarters of the Center for National Studies, which is considered the first of its kind in the Republic.
