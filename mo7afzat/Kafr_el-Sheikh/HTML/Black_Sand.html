Black Sand



Hundreds of people flock from all Egyptian governorates to see the black sand dunes scattered on the Mediterranean coast from Burj Al-Burlus to the east of the Baltim resort, which are full of heavy metals in the black sand in many areas, and the percentage of them found ranges between 1% to 7%, but this percentage may reach In the Burullus region to about 80%, which confirms the very high economic return from the optimal use of this black sand.

This area is considered a source of economic importance that can bring millions of dollars to Egypt, pointing out that its exploitation saves us millions of hard currencies that the state spends in importing minerals such as zircon, which is one of the main elements in the ceramic industry in which Egypt thrives, in addition to Other minerals, including iron, granite, monazite, and titanium, which are used in the manufacture of aircraft hulls, are unfortunately exposed to thefts.
