Giza Pyramids

You cannot leave Egypt without visiting the Pyramids of Giza, which is one of the Seven Wonders of the World and was built 25 century BC. The well-known Giza pyramids consist of three main pyramids (Khufu, Khafre, and Menkaure). From the inside, the mummification of kings and a number of antiquities and rare pieces, in addition to the presence of a number of hieroglyphic writings that fill the walls from the inside, besides, there is the amazing architectural masterpiece (the Sphinx), which represents King Khafre, combining the strength of the lion and the wisdom of man, and there are A number of archaeological remains scattered in this place, and whether you are a fan of history or archeology or not, you must visit this impressive historical site.

Sphinx

The most famous Egyptian landmark in the world beside the pyramids, is the well-known stone Sphinx, located in Giza in the Cairo governorate, which attracts the eyes with its unique design, with the body of an animal and a human head. About 4,500 years ago, many tourists go to it to take beautiful memorial photos, and contemplate the details of its construction and its large size, as it is considered the largest archaeological statue in the world.

The Grand Egyptian Museum

The Egyptian Museum is one of the most important museums in Cairo that you must visit, as it displays a wide and large collection of antiquities of the Pharaonic civilization, with more than 136 thousand pieces. In addition to a good number of antiquities in its stores, the museum contains a large number of sections, including the mummification and mummy section, and a section containing collections of treasures from Pharaonic tombs that were discovered from a number of tombs, the most important of which is the tomb of Tutankhamun, as well as the statue. The only one of King Khufu. In the vicinity of the museum, you will find many wonderful souvenir shops that contain many statues and distinctive gifts that you can buy for your family or friends as a special gift from Arab Egypt.

Khufu Ship Museum

The Khufu Ship Museum, which is located at the foot of the Great Pyramid of Giza, is a distinguished museum, as it includes a group of ancient artifacts and a group of ancient Pharaonic boats, known as (the sun boats), which the pharaohs used to bury near their tombs, because of their belief that they They will need a way to pass into the afterlife from after death. In 1954, boats made of cedar wood were found near the pyramid of Khufu, which is now the site of the museum, and are displayed inside it after the parts of the boat, which numbered 1,200 pieces, were assembled. It is a distinctive museum that reflects the culture of the ancient pharaohs, visit it and learn about this ancient culture with your family or friends.

Pharaonic Village

The Pharaonic Village embodies time travel in itself, where history and art come from Egypt to revive the time of the pharaohs again. Honestly and close to the Pharaonic period, in addition to that, the architectural character of the village, with its temples and palaces decorated in the ancient Egyptian Pharaonic language, will make you dive into the enchanting beauty of those golden periods of the history of this great country, and you can move between them by boat. The village contains a number of restaurants and recreational areas that you can sit in after your tour around it.

Dahshur pyramids

The village of Dahshur is located about 40 kilometers southwest of Cairo, and represents the southern end of the broad pyramid field that begins at Giza. Excavations at Dahshur have revealed the remains of seven pyramids, as well as extensive tomb complexes built for queens and nobles. Today, Dahshur also houses two of the best-preserved pyramids in Egypt, both built during the reign of King Sneferu. The curved pyramid and the red pyramid in Dahshur are a tremendous evidence of the architectural development that led to the construction of the Great Pyramid and its dependencies in Giza.

Saqqara pyramids

You must have heard about the Pyramids of Giza, but you will be glad to know that Egypt also has many other great pyramids that you will enjoy exploring. Just a few kilometers from Cairo, the Pyramids of Saqqara offer an insight into the architectural talent that flourished among the ancient Egyptians. The red pyramid, the curved pyramid, and the stepped pyramid together make up the stunning Saqqara pyramids that stand tall in front of their visitors, and there are also many ancient tombs with intricately decorated interior walls that will catch your eye along with the pyramids.

Valley Temple of Khafre

Among the three pyramids and the famous Sphinx, lies a distinctive archaeological landmark, which occupied an important historical place in the Pharaonic civilization, is the Valley Temple of Khafre, which was built at the time of the pharaohs along the river, and the temple was established to be the mummification site of the pharaoh after his death, before it was transferred to the pyramids, It is placed in the sarcophagus, and when visiting the pyramids, visitors can tour the temple, and see its gate, which is still standing.
