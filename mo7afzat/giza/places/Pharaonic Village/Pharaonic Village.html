DISCOVER THE BEST OF "Pharaonic Village"



The Pharaonic Village was founded by Dr. Hassan Rajab d. An interesting fact, that he was also the one who rediscovered the ancient Egyptian art of paper making (papyrus).

Pharaonic Village:

The Pharaonic Village is a historical experience not to be missed. It is a unique place where the entire history of Egypt is explained in 2-3 hours, from ancient life to modern times. The Pharaonic Village is a great introduction to Egypt, and an ideal stop for anyone's first trip to Egypt.. It is located on an island in the Nile, 3 miles south of central Cairo. In this village, you are taken by floating stands, while a hundred actors and actresses show scenes from ancient Egypt (papyrus making, carving, house building...etc). It is truly a unique experience that you cannot find anywhere else. This trip is definitely one your kids will always remember!

Journey to the past:

The trip begins with entering the village and buying tickets from the ticket office at the gate, then the reception buoy. It contains a small cafeteria to receive visitors and an exhibition of some archaeological and pharaonic products. On the top floor of the reception buoy there is the Cleopatra Museum, where the tour guide explains to you about the reign of Cleopatra and Julius Caesar And the struggle that took place in that era, after seeing the Cleopatra Museum, your journey begins inside the village since you board the floating boat. The methods of manufacturing it and converting it into paper, an overview of the methods of mummification, papermaking, pottery, glass and weapons, information about the methods of agriculture, irrigation and hunting at the Pharaohs, methods of drawing and hieroglyphic writing, all this information you hear and watch representative scenes that mimic the past, to bring the picture closer and simulate the life of the ancient Egyptians and all the details of their lives.

Museums in the Pharaonic Village:

The most distinctive feature of the Pharaonic village after the trip by boat is the various museums that deal with all the history of ancient and modern Egypt. The Coptic Museum, the Mummification Museum, the Museum of Modern Egypt, the Napoleon Museum, and the Cleopatra Museum, each of which deals with a historical era and is supported by pictures and miniature mockups of the most important events, battles, clothes and tools used during that era of Egypt’s history.

President Gamal Abdel Nasser Museum:

Gamal Abdel Nasser Antiques is a museum that deals with the life of the late Egyptian President Gamal Abdel Nasser from his birth until his death in the early seventies. The museum is located in the pharaonic village on the banks of the Nile. The museum contains more than 170 rare pictures of President Gamal Abdel Nasser in the various stages of his life. Abdel Nasser during the years 1941 and 1942, in addition to the text of the decision to nationalize the Suez Canal on July 26, 1956, his abdication speech on June 9, 1956, and the medical report on Nasser's death.

Museum of former President Mohamed Anwar Sadat:

The museum contains a large collection of personal belongings, photos, and some personal belongings that Mrs. Jihan Sadat gifted to the museum, such as his “marine suit” in which he attended the opening ceremony of the Suez Canal, his own jacket, his famous stick, toothpaste, toothbrush and perfume. It also contains a collection of rare memorial photos of Sadat on various occasions, including the picture of his wives and children, and during moments of reflection on his life, such as a picture of his resting place in the pyramid and a picture of him performing ablution and praying. Al-Ramli, and pictures of him with the most important international figures during the signing of the famous peace agreement at Camp David.

Mummification Museum:

It contains some examples of the mummification process and the most important deities associated with the idea of ​​mummification, and the Pyramids Museum, which represents the idea of ​​building the pyramid, the most important tools used in its construction, obelisks...etc.

A first-class pharaonic tour:

One of the most beautiful and interesting parts of the trip was the tour inside the house of the noble or rich man of the pharaohs, and the tour inside the house of the poor man, for even the days of the pharaohs, Egypt did not know social justice. And a large area in front of the house, while the poor man's house is from one room, and his poor wife sits to grind the wheat and prepare the flour for long hours, while the rich man's wife spends all her time listening to the harp and applying cosmetics!

Some fun for the kids:

Inside the village there is an arts center, in which some artistic activities are held, such as art workshops for glass, pottery and papyrus making. There is also a place dedicated to games, such as mini amusement parks, where young people can play if they are bored of watching the various museums, and there is also a specialized center for photography.

Village products:

Part of the tour inside the pharaonic village includes visiting the market and the bazaar, which offers many different pharaonic goods and products, which visitors eagerly accept, as well as the village studio, where they can photograph you in pharaonic clothes and in a pharaonic atmosphere, and filming is not allowed inside the bazaar or the studio, and there is also A section dedicated to the Pharaonic market selling glass products, perfumes, copper and leather products, sand painting, henna drawings, and some traditional and pharaonic artifacts.
