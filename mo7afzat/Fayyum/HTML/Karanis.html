Karanis



The ancient city of Karanis in Fayoum Governorate is located in the Kom Ushim area on the Cairo-Fayoum road at km 70. It is one of the Greco-Roman villages established by Ptolemy II. It has an area of 800 meters in width. The area of the residential city of Karanis is about one kilometer from east to west and about 800 m from north to The eastern neighborhood was famous for several streets, including the Royal Street, which was revealed by the excavations of the mission of the Faculty of Arts. It was Karanis and on the edge of one of the most fertile areas in Egypt, which is Fayoum, where wheat, barley, dates, olives and fruits were abundantly planted.

Ahmed Hassan Mustafa, an antiquities inspector in the Fayoum antiquities area, said that the city of Karanis, com Oshim, has a temple that was built in the first century AD, and in front of it there is a large basin outside the temple that was used to raise crocodiles, then the temple, which begins with a longitudinal hall, and has a group of rooms on the northern and southern sides, with stairs To go up to the upper floor, which has now completely disappeared, and there is a transverse hall before the Holy of Holies that was used to place the crocodiles, then the Holy of Holies, which is a large place “mstafa” used to place the mummified statue. On both sides of the room there was a group of rooms used to store the valuables of the temple and offerings Which was presented by the common people to the temple, and the temple was used to worship the god Sobek, who was embodied in the form of a crocodile, and his worship spread in Fayoum from the beginning of the first dynasty until the end of the Roman era and the beginning of the entry of Christianity into Egypt.

He added that the Genoese and northern temples are devoid of decorations and were built in the first century AD, and in the front of the temple there is an inscription directed by the Roman governor of Egypt dedicating the inscription from Emperor Nero to the god Sobek, in addition to another inscription in the gate adjacent to the temple from Emperor Fasvian to the god Sobek.
