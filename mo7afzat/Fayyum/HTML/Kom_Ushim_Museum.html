Kom Ushim Museum



Archaeological statues that smell the scent of the ancient Egyptian civilization and the greatness of our ancestors' history, and distinctive art paintings that are thousands of years old and tell different stories and legends... all inside a distinguished museum in the middle of a desert area on the Cairo-Fayoum desert road in the Kom Oshim area.

The Kom Oshim Museum in Fayoum Governorate, which was opened last month, is located at the entrance to the famous city of Karanis, and is one of the most important museums in Egypt; It consists of two floors and contains 320 different artifacts. The museum was rehabilitated with self-financing from the Ministry of Antiquities. The restoration and development work included raising the iron fence around it with a height of 3 meters, constructing guard towers, changing the lighting system, installing surveillance cameras, and changing the paints of the facades. And the preparation and development of two museum displays, and the museum’s space was increased in 1993, when a loft was attached to it, and in 2006 the museum was closed to carry out its restoration work, which began at the beginning of this year.

Elham Salah, Head of the Museums Sector at the Ministry of Antiquities, said that the museum contains 320 artifacts, some of which are displayed for the first time to the public.
