Lahun Pyramid



The Lahoun Pyramid is one of the pyramids of Egypt, built by King Senusret II of the 12th Dynasty, of mud bricks over a 12-meter-high hill on the outskirts of the city of Lahoun (Fayoum Governorate), which is 22 kilometers from the city of Fayoum.

The pyramid was covered with limestone and was 48 meters high and 106 meters long. Its entrance is located on the southern side, unlike the rest of the Egyptian pyramids.

Inside it was found the golden uraeus that was placed above the royal crown, and next to it were the terraces of the tomb of Princess Sat Hathora Ayunt and the tomb of the pyramid engineer Enppi in the south, and 8 terraces that were the tombs of members of the royal family, and in the pyramid area there are the Lahoun cemetery and the city of Lahoun workers.

Sayed Al-Shura, Director General of the Fayoum Antiquities District, says that Senusret II is the fourth king of the Twelfth Dynasty from the nineteenth century BC. With the beginning of the Middle Kingdom, the capital of Egypt moved to an area close to Lahoun at the entrance to Fayoum, which was called “East Kay”, meaning holding on the two lands. , because it controls Egypt from the north and south, and very large irrigation projects have been carried out and the construction of the pyramids has begun, including the pyramid of Lahun and the pyramid of Hawara.

While Dr. Nabil Hazal, a tourist advisor in Fayoum Governorate, stated that the Lahun pyramid was built in the nineteenth century BC, and was discovered by an English archaeologist and entered it for the first time, where he found a table of offerings and a golden uraeus cobra placed on the royal crown, then closed the pyramid, to be opened today To visit and be an archaeological attraction after a project for restoration, development and removal of rubble, noting that he was able for the first time to enter Senusret II's burial chamber.

Ahmed Abdel-Al, the former Director General of the Fayoum Antiquities District, explained that the Lahoun pyramid is about 22 kilometers from the city of Fayoum, and it was built by Senusret II, one of the kings of the Twelfth Dynasty, of mud bricks, on a high hill 12 meters high overlooking the village of Lahoun in Fayoum. Its height is 48 meters, its base is 106 meters long, and it was covered with limestone. In 1889, the English archaeologist William Flinders Petrie opened the pyramid and found inside it the only golden uraeus that was placed above the royal crown, and it is now in the Egyptian Museum in Tahrir, and the tomb of the princess was revealed.
