Dimai El-Sebaa

The Lions of El-Sebaa is close to Lake Qarun, which is the archaeological ruins of an ancient Roman city where many stone statues appear, the most famous of which are the two statues of “Crouching Lions” made of sandstone.
In addition to this, there is an ancient temple from the Greek era called the “Temple of the God Sknobios”, and visitors to that area can practice the hobby of hunting wild birds.



Karanis

Greek antiquities are not confined to the governorate of Alexandria only, but the ancient city of Cranes is one of the most prominent Greco-Roman villages in Fayoum, where all its traces indicate the life of ancient Egyptian families under the rule of Ptolemy II.



Kom Ushim Museum

The Kom Oshim Archeology Museum combines various antiquity and modern times, both Coptic and Islamic. This museum also owns more than 300 artifacts, each of which varies in shape and history.
These pieces were distributed on the two floors of the museum, where the first floor was dedicated to displaying the daily life of man in those eras, through antiques of household, agricultural and other crafts.
As for the second floor, it displays the history and tools of the ancient funerary world.



Lahun Pyramid

The pyramid of Lahoun has attracted hundreds of tourists since its reopening in 2019, as the inner corridor of the pyramid was revealed, which visitors to the area can enter and view the interior rooms and coffins in it.
In addition, the geographical location of the pyramid is distinguished, which is far from the capital of ancient Egyptian civilization. There are also some remains of the funerary city of Lahoun and some other monuments related to King Senusret II.



Lake Qarun

It is one of the oldest Egyptian lakes that derives its water from the flooding of the Nile River, and has now become a destination for many visitors under the slogan “one-day resort”.
Lake Qarun has many advantages that make it one of the most important places of recreation in Fayoum, especially the surrounding beaches and the river tours that are held there, in addition to the nearby archaeological sites such as the Ptolemaic village of Dimiya El Sebaa.



Medinet Madi

In the same context as the pyramid of the Lahun, King Amenemhat III decided to establish this ancient city, and made it one of the most important centers for the manufacture of statues in the ancient Pharaonic time, and the seat of the worship of the gods at that time.



Qarun Palace

Qarun Palace was not only very popular among tourists and residents of the city, but some circulated many false facts, sometimes, about it in order to glorify its position. Therefore, Qaroun Palace is one of the most important places in Fayoum that tourists look forward to visiting on their way to the El-behera.



Tunisia Village El-Fayyom

Although the village was established about 30 years ago, it was not outdated, but rather became more beautiful and became more popular.
This is the village of Tunis in Fayoum Governorate, which consists of a group of rustic-style houses made of mud and domes, surrounded by trees on one side, and Lake Qarun on the other.



Wadi El-Hitan(Whale Valley)

Do you like Scouting trips? The idea of going to an open arena depends on your physical skills, with your peers participating in that trip by roaming around the place and discovering some clues about it!
Here in Wadi Al-Hitan in Fayoum, you will be able to embark on an unparalleled scouting adventure, where you will encounter the first facts about this place as it is an ancient ocean floor, and a refuge for many rare wild animals, most notably the “whales” whose fossils in the valley amounted to more than 400 skeletons.
UNESCO classified it as the best global area for great whale skeletons!



Wadi El-Rayan

Wadi El Rayan gives a new and different concept to the desert environment, as many roads have been paved inside it to accommodate the movement of thousands of tourists there, in addition to the services and facilities available to serve visitors.
In Wadi El Rayan, there are many natural features that are unique to the city of Fayoum, such as: sand dunes, migratory birds, wild animals, and finally water springs.
