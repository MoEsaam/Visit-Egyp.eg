Wadi Degla Reserve



Wadi Degla is located east of Maadi neighborhood in the Eastern Desert in Cairo Governorate and is considered one of the important valleys that extend from east to west with a length of about 30 km.

It passes through limestone rocks that were deposited in the marine environment in the Eastern Desert (60 million years ago), so it is rich in fossils.

History



The Tigris Valley is considered part of the northern plateau of limestone rocks of the Eocene era, which Al-Qassassi defined in 1993 as a distinctive environment with a geographical nature in Egypt.

This type of environment is not represented in the nature reserves unit except for the Petrified Forest Reserve.
