King Tut Museum

The King Tut Museum in Genena City is one of the most important tourist attractions in South Sinai and is visited by many tourists from all over the world. It includes a collection of one of the groups of rare pieces from the tomb of King Tutankhamun of Egypt, where all the pieces were brought under the supervision of the Ministry of Antiquities Affairs. The museum includes 135 pieces in an integrated collection with a series of photos showing the opening of the tomb and some handwritten documents. The tomb was discovered in 1922 AD, and the museum was designed to the highest level of illumination. Do not hesitate and visit it with your family and friends and enjoy learning about the amazing pharaonic civilization!

St. Catherine's Monastery

The Greek Orthodox Monastery of Saint Catherine is one of the oldest places of worship in the world. Founded in the fourth century, it is located on the site where Moses is believed to have seen the burning bush. It is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The walled monastery complex includes a fortress, main church, Transfiguration Church and monastic buildings dating back To the sixth century, along with the library of famous manuscripts, priceless icons, collectibles and famous artworks, Mount Sinai is located 2,285 meters above the monastery, and it is possible to climb to the top where Moses received the Ten Commandments, and from there you will be rewarded with stunning views of the monastery. and surroundings.
