Date of establishment of Ras Mohammed Reserve:

Ras Mohamed Reserve was the first nature reserve to be established in Egypt, and it was officially declared a nature reserve in 1938 AD, on an area of ​​97 km. km of wild land, and 345 km of coral reefs and an aquatic environment.

reason of calling:

There is more than one opinion about the reason for calling the Ras Muhammad Protectorate by this name, some say that it was called so because it appears on the map as a triangular head whose base consists of mountain ranges in southern Sinai, while others say that it looks like the head of a man with a huge beard, with its water and land parts, so he called it a reserve Ras Muhammad.

Ras Muhammed Reserve .. Unique location and diversity:

Ras Mohammed Reserve in South Sinai is considered the second most important nature reserve in the world, over the past thirty years, after Uluru-Kata Park Tjuta in Australia. Ras Mohammed Reserve is located at the confluence of the Gulf of Suez and the Gulf of Aqaba. The eastern edge of the Ras Mohammed Reserve represents a rocky wall with the waters of the Gulf in which coral reefs are located. Ras Mohammed area is characterized by coral beaches located in the depths of the waters of Ras Mohammed, colorful fish, endangered sea turtles and rare aquatic organisms. Ras Muhammed National Park... the destination of divers:

Ras Mohammed Reserve includes more than 12 beaches and is considered a destination for divers in the world because it combines three environments in a wonderful assortment of marine algae, coral reefs and fish (Picasso fish with bright colors). Ras Mohamed Reserve is considered the most important nature reserve in Egypt according to the international classification, because it is one of the 3 most important diving sites in the world, due to the quality of coral reefs and the marine environment in them, in addition to the quality of coral reefs in Egypt in general, which is not rivaled by any country except Australia.

Biological diversity in the reserve:

There are many species in the reserve, including:
Birds, such as: hawks, egrets, storks, mammals such as: foxes, hyenas, mountain rabbits, deer, and mountain goats. - Marine animals, such as: dolphins, sharks, and sea turtles, and there are about 150 species of coral reefs.
