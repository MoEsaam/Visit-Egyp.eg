DISCOVER THE BEST OF "Sharm El Sheikh Museum"



Sharm El Sheikh Museum:

Sharm El-Sheikh Museum is the first museum of Egyptian antiquities in Sharm El-Sheikh, which is world-famous for beach tourism, to be a center of cultural and civilizational radiation and a meeting place for human civilizations, and a tangible addition to promising tourism in the city, in line with the ministry’s plan to integrate recreational tourism and cultural tourism, so that visitors and tourists can enjoy the picturesque beaches Enjoying the marine and wilderness of the city and at the same time getting to know the ancient Egyptian civilization. The idea of ​​establishing the museum dates back to 1999, and it is located on an area of ​​191,000 square meters. Work on the project began in 2003, then stopped in 2011, to be resumed again in 2018, at a cost of 812 million pounds. The museum includes three museum halls, in addition to To a recreational area that includes a number of restaurants, bazaars, traditional craft shops, an open theater and arenas for holding celebrations and events.

Museum show scenario:

The scenario of the museum display of the Sharm El-Sheikh Museum deals with the emergence and development of the Egyptian civilization, as well as sheds light on the Egyptian person and his behavior towards the environment around him, and how to coexist with its sanctified creatures. Suez, Ismailia, the Greco-Roman in Alexandria, the Egyptian Museum in Tahrir, as well as the stores of Luxor and Ashmounin in Minya and others, to be displayed inside the museum’s various halls, namely the Great Hall, the Hathor corridor, the cemetery area, or in the external display area.

Museum halls:

The Great Hall expresses man and wildlife in ancient Egypt, by displaying a number of paintings representing the Egyptian family in different historical stages. In ancient Egypt, such as cats and scarabs of different shapes and sizes, which were discovered in 2019 in the Saqqara archaeological area. As for the Hathor corridor, it was called by this name due to the presence of a large number of pieces belonging to the god Hathor, along with some statues of Kings Thutmose I, Hatshepsut, Nectanebo, Ramses II, and Ramses III. It also displays a simulation of a complete archaeological tomb with all its details and the funerary furniture it contains.

The most important artifacts on display:

Among the most important artifacts in the museum is a rare piece of mosaic from Alexandria dating back to the fourth century BC, a statue of the god “Eros” hunting deer, which is displayed at the entrance to the museum, and another rare statue of the god “Bes” made of incompletely burned clay that is displayed inside the great hall. In addition to the pieces that were recently discovered in some archaeological sites for display for the first time, thus enriching the museum's display system. The museum also exceptionally displays 10 artifacts from the treasures of King Tutankhamun, before displaying them at the Grand Egyptian Museum. Attracting visitors to the city to see part of the treasures of the young king for the first time, and a path for people with special needs has been set up in the museum.

working hours:

In line with the policy of the Ministry of Tourism and Antiquities to integrate beach tourism with cultural tourism; It was decided to open the museum throughout the week for two periods, one of which is in the morning from 10 am to 1 pm, and the other period is in the evening from 5 pm to 11 pm, as these dates will provide an opportunity for tourists to enjoy the picturesque beaches of the city and practice fun water sports in the morning, and visit the museum At night to learn about the ancient Egyptian civilization.
