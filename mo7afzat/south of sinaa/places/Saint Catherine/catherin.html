Saint Catherine (city):

Saint Catherine is the most special and distinguished city in Sinai. It is the highest inhabited place in Sinai. It is located on a plateau that rises 1,600 meters above sea level in the heart of South Sinai, 300 km from the Suez Canal, with an area of ​​5130 square km, surrounded by a group of mountains is the highest In Sinai and in all of Egypt, the highest peaks are Mount Catherine, Mount Musa, and Mount Safsafa. This altitude gave it a distinct climate, moderate in summer and very cold in winter, which gives it a special beauty when snow covers the tops of the mountains and the land of the city. The area has been declared a nature reserve due to its natural, historical and religious significance. Most of the city's residents work in agriculture, grazing and tourism services. The city is famous for religious tourism, safari tourism and mountain climbing, and there is St. Catherine's Monastery, Mount Moses, the shrine of the Prophet Aaron and other religious monuments, and it is considered the largest natural reserve in the Arab Republic of Egypt in terms of area.

naming:

The city was named after Saint Catherine (St. Catherine), who was born in Alexandria at the end of the third century AD and was described as wise, rational and modest. She was raised on the love of Christ, attended schools, and was educated in the sciences of the Bible. When she was eighteen, she completed the study of theology and philosophy and was baptized. In the year 307 AD, Caesar Maximian came to Alexandria, and he ordered the worship of idols, persecuted the saint, and tortured her until he ordered her beheaded on November 25, 307 AD. Five centuries after her martyrdom, a monk in Sinai saw a group of angels carrying her body, flying with it, and placing it on the top of a mountain in Sinai. The monastery built by Emperor Justinian in the sixth century AD. The monastery was known as Saint Catherine's Monastery, and the name was given to the whole city.

Saint Catherine Sanctuary:

St. Catherine Reserve is located at the end of the junction of Wadi Al-Isba’iya with Wadi Al-Arba’een on a high plateau surrounded by high altitudes represented by several mountains of varying heights, namely Mount St. Catherine, the highest peak in Egypt, Mount Moses, Mount Safsafa, Mount Al-Sana’a, Jabal Al-Banat and Jabal Abbas. Climbing on it without specific pestles, the reserve possesses a unique cultural heritage represented in the monastery of St. Catherine with its architectural contents and artistic and archaeological treasures, and the sacred mountains around it of religious significance as well as some other religious monuments such as the tomb of the Prophet Saleh and the tomb of Aaron. The area was declared a nature reserve in 1988, with an area of ​​about 4,300 square kilometers. Zaytuna and Well of Aaron. The reserve is rich in many forms of wildlife, such as foxes, hyenas, tattles, deer, ibex, hares, wolves, Arab hedgehogs, spiny mice, jerboas and reptiles such as tresha, as well as various types of birds, the most important of which are storks, eagles, falcons, eagles, kestrels, snarls, crowned cats, lunar cats, butler's owls, grouse, camels, tamar, raven, sparrow and sparrow.

mountains:

The city is spread across landscapes, including mountains and valleys, that make walking in it a pleasure for the soul, which led to the spread of safari trips in the city for those seeking nature’s pleasure. Among the most famous mountains in the region is Jabal al-Banat, which is a great mountain towards Suryal, separated by Wadi Ferran, and Jabal Musa, which is about 7,363 feet above sea level. On its summit is a small church, and next to it is a small mosque. As for Mount St. Catherine, it is one of the highest mountains in Egypt, with a height of 8,563 feet above sea level. It was so named because, according to the traditions of the monks, the angels in the past carried the body of St. Catherine from the place of her martyrdom in Alexandria in the year 307 AD. It was brought to this mountain, and only the skull and the bone of one of the hands remain, which are preserved in two boxes inside the church, and one who climbs the top of the mountain can see within sight the Gulf of Aqaba and the Gulf of Suez.
