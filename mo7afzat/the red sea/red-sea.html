Marsa Alam reefs

Marsa Alam Reefs, this exciting coral reef, is located 25 minutes from Marsa Alam. Here you will find wrecks, cave systems, and one of the most beautiful mountain reef gardens. The length of these reefs ranges from 350 meters to 400 meters in the shape of the letter L, and it constitutes one of the most beautiful natural art paintings. Here, various diving trips are organized in search of beauty!

Mini Egypt Park

Mini Egypt Park is a miniature park that extends over an open area that displays buildings and models that simulate the most beautiful and ancient buildings in the world. Here you will find miniature statues of the pyramids, the Temple of Abu Simbel and the High Dam in Aswan, passing through the Temple of Edfu to the legendary Luxor of the famous Karnak Temple, in addition to a number of landmarks It was built of stones and boulders.

Sand City Museum

The Sand City Museum is dedicated to carved sand and is the only museum of its kind in Africa and the Middle East. The Sand City in Hurghada consists of 42 historical statues and 17 restored masterpieces belonging to artists from different countries who left part of the heart and soul in their work. The most important feature of this museum is that everything it displays is located outside (ie in the open air) and is close to hotels and commercial centers. Visit it with your family or friends and enjoy seeing all the amazing statues and works in it, where you will see miniature pyramids, dragon figures, Cleopatra and many more.

Giftun Island

Giftun Island in Hurghada is the only island in Egypt where tourist activities are permitted, and it is the first natural reserve in the Red Sea. Giftun Island is home to seagulls, and Giftun Island is characterized by its crystal clear waters and beautiful beaches.

Hurghada Grand Aquarium

The Grand Aquarium, located in the south of Hurghada, is considered one of the most important tourist attractions in the Red Sea, and one of the icons of Hurghada's tourist attractions.

The Ottoman Qusair Castle "Al-Tabiah"

It was known as Al-Tabia, a fortress established by the Ottomans 220 years ago to protect the convoys of pilgrims sailing to and from the lands of the Hijaz, and with the passage of time it became an archaeological place reached by several tourist visits.
