About the Sand Museum:

The idea of ​​the Sand Museum is unique, as it is the first of its kind in the Middle East and North Africa. What gave this museum distinction is that it collects 42 statues that embody many civilizations, and all the statues were made and carved from sand by more than 40 international sculptors from different countries, including Russia, Turkey, Germany, America and others. About 12,000 tons of sand were used, in addition to 15,000 tons of water, which took 7 months to finish.

What are the statues made in the Sand Museum:

The sculptors used Egyptian sands known as yellow sands and also white sands. These sands are characterized by low salinity, and to increase the preservation of the statues, they put an adhesive substance before placing the sand cubes, which in turn prevents sand from falling. There are ongoing periodic restoration work for these statues by technicians and experts because of the weather changes they are exposed to.

Why is Hurghada Sand Museum an important place for tourists?

The Sand Museum in Hurghada includes 42 carefully carved statues of many different monuments, also of historical figures as well as legendary figures from the ancient era as well as the modern era. Such as the statue of Napoleon Bonaparte, Queen Isis, Queen Cleopatra, Alexander the Great, and Sultan Mehmed the Conqueror. In addition to the presence of sand models of the pyramids and the Sphinx. There are also many cultural landmarks around the world. Like the Indian legend Lavokavirita. It also collects many cartoon figurines that are loved by all children of our time. Like superheroes Batman and Spiderman. Also, because there is no similar museum to it in the Middle East, it is the first of its kind, which has made it an important and attractive place for tourism. There are also many restaurants within it that serve the most delicious and delicious foods. In addition to rest stops for tourists to provide comfort, as well as places for children, which help the whole family to enjoy.
