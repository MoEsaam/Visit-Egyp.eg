Umm al-Rakhm



Among its most famous tourist attractions: Agiba Plateau, Agiba Beach, Al-Abyad Beach, the Temple of Ramses II, and there are a number of tourist villages.

Temple of Ramses II

It was built by Ramses II in Umm al-Rakhm south of the Agiba plateau. It was discovered by the Egyptian archaeologist Labib Habashi in the year 1942 AD. He found wonderful hieroglyphic inscriptions in it. Next to it were found the ruins of the castle built by Ramses II, surrounded by the remains of the stone wall that was built to protect Egypt from the attacks of Libyan tribes.

Agiba Beac

It is characterized by caves and very beautiful rock formations, where this beach is characterized by its view of a plateau high from the sea.
