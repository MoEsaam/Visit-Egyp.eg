Cleopatra's Baths



If they tell you that history only immortalizes the names of those who had a share of wars, tell them about Cleopatra, the beauty queen...

Here in Matrouh, 9 km west of the city, is the Cleopatra Bath. The attractive aesthetic appearance of Kilauea Batra bath is due to the fact that it is a huge rock closed from the top, and it has holes that allow sunlight to penetrate into it and warm the water inside that rock formation. This is in addition to the presence of openings on the side that allow the entry of water when the tidal movement, but the tidal movements may cause frightening sounds inside!

It is said that Queen Cleopatra used it for sunbathing and swimming or as a way to preserve her skin. The ruins of the queen's palace were found on a high place opposite the bath.

Taking a souvenir photo in this place is a very beautiful thing ... but beware of the flow of water because it may be dangerous in certain places.
