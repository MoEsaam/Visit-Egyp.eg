Military Museum



The foundation of Port-Said Military Museum dates back to 1964, and commemorates the tripartite aggression on Port-Said city in 1956. The museum was inaugurated on the national day of Port-Said governorate on the 23rd of December, 1964. The museum went through renovations, maintenance and development and was reopened in 1978, after attaching an additional hall for displaying the collections of October War, then was reopened a second time on the 23rd of December, 2009. The museum targets documenting the acts of the tripartite aggression on Port-Said city, and witnessing the greatness and heroism of the people of Port-Said while defending their land. The museum was established on a space of 7000 square meters. The museum includes a garden for open-air museum displays, across from which lies the main construction which includes the display halls, the facilities, and the museum services.

The museum recites the epic of Port-Said people's persistence and valor, while resisting the tripartite aggression of 1956, by displaying various collections of weaponry and equipment, which were used or captured during the 1956 and 1973 wars. The museum is divided into three sections, open-air showground, the permanent display showroom, and the temporary display showroom.

Open-air show-ground presents a group of heavy weapons, equipment and some reconnaissance aircraft which were seized during 1956 and 1973 wars, in addition to some antiquarian arms and cannons that date back to Mohamed Ali's era.

Permanent Galleries are divided into two sections, the main lobby and three halls (Suez canal hall – tripartite aggression hall – October-1973 hall):
Main Lobby narrates the history of Port-Said starting from the ancient Egyptian eras, passing by the Islamic era, and up until the modern age, through displaying works of art, which give the visitor valuable historical information about Port-Said city.
Suez Canal Gallery displays a group of collections that illustrates the story of digging the Suez Canal, starting from the planning phase until the finishing point, and also displays a group of historical documents belonging to the execution and opening phases of the Suez Canal project, the critical role played by the canal in the political, military, and economical history of Egypt, and the nationalization decision of 1956. There is also a Diorama for the digging works of Suez Canal, and another one for the opening, and recently another Diorama has been made for the New Suez Canal project.
1956-Tripartite-Aggression Gallery includes an amazing set of artworks which demonstrate the bravery of Port-Said people, in addition to portraits of the popular resistance figures. It also displays some samples of weapons and personal possessions of the soldiers who fought in the tripartite aggression. The hall displays an artistic Diorama that tells about some major battles such as Algameel and Hay Almonakh battles.
October-1973 Gallery This hall was established during the museum development phase in 1978. It displays some artworks illustrating the Armed Forces valiant actions during the resistance and attrition phases and during October War, showing some of the Israeli small arms captured in the time of War, in addition to a special display of a set of Dioramas that tells about major battles such as the Tank battle, Ras Al-Esh battle and an incursion into a strong point.
